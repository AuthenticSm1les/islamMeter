# Islamic Identity and Values Assessment Questions

Please rate the following statements on a scale of:
++ (Strongly Agree) | + (Agree) | = (Neutral) | - (Disagree) | -- (Strongly Disagree)

### Dimension 1: Identity (Foundational Belief)
1. I feel a deep personal commitment to the core dogmas of Islam.
2. My sense of self is fundamentally anchored in my belief in the divine.
3. Whether others see me as Muslim is less important than my internal conviction.
4. My religious identity is the most important factor in my personal life.
5. I would maintain my faith even if I were completely isolated from other Muslims.

### Dimension 2: Compliance (Attitude toward Rules)
6. When I fail to follow an Islamic rule, I view it as my own personal struggle (sin).
7. I believe the rules of Islam are fixed, even if I struggle to live up to them.
8. I don't believe in "reinterpreting" rules just to make them easier to follow.
9. I feel a sense of guilt when my actions do not align with Islamic teachings.
10. I believe that Islamic laws are timeless and not subject to change based on cultural trends.

### Dimension 3: Political Method (Governance/Coercion)
11. I believe Islamic values should only be promoted through social influence, not force.
12. I am opposed to any form of revolutionary or coerced imposition of faith.
13. I believe in working within existing civic/democratic systems to advocate for values.
14. I believe that religious influence in politics should respect the rights of all citizens.
15. I am fundamentally against the use of violence or intimidation to achieve religious goals.

### Dimension 4: Cultural (Community & Heritage)
16. I feel a strong sense of belonging to the global Muslim community (Ummah).
17. I participate in Islamic cultural traditions because they are part of my heritage.
18. I feel a sense of comfort being around other Muslims regardless of their piety.
19. I value the traditions and customs of my culture even when they are distinct from strict religious dogma.
20. Celebrating community festivals is an essential part of maintaining my identity.

### Dimension 5: Authority (Tradition vs. Personal Interpretation)
21. I believe the guidance of traditional scholarship is essential for understanding Islam.
22. I prefer relying on established consensus rather than personal interpretation.
23. I am skeptical of "modernized" versions of faith that deviate from tradition.
24. I believe that learning from qualified scholars is the most reliable path to knowledge.
25. I trust historical interpretations of texts more than contemporary perspectives.

### Dimension 6: Public Sphere (Private vs. Public)
26. I believe Islamic ethics should play a visible role in shaping public law.
27. I think a society functions best when its laws are informed by religious moral frameworks.
28. I believe religion should not be relegated solely to the private, individual sphere.
29. I believe that public policy should reflect the moral values derived from my faith.
30. I see no conflict between living a modern civic life and advocating for religious morality in the public space.
